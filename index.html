Winning by Electoral Colleges

The current system of the American presidential elections uses the Electoral College which delegates points per state. If a candidate wins majority of voters in that state, all the state's points are awarded to the candidate, in other words: 'winner takes all'. The winner is decided once a candidate earns more than half of the available points.

With this system, Biden won comfortably with 306 points.

Winning by popular vote

It is commonly debated that the presidential elections should be determined by popular vote, because it is simpler to understand and represents the choice of the people. Whereas swing states and large states receive more favors due to the Electoral College's 'winner takes all' system.

As we can see Biden wins this scenario by 8 million votes.

Winning by number of counties won

This unfairness is clearly shown in this choropleth map, where Trump seemingly won the most counties. Although Trump has won the most counties and has won by a landslide, but every county has different populations. Los Angeles County in California has roughly 10 million people, which is an incredible difference to Loving County in Texas with 169 people.

Through this system, Trump crushes Biden by more than 2000 counties.

Winning by state population density

To balance the discrepancy between counties, we will instead consider the population density per state. This should not only balance each state, but also the winner should receive the most popular vote.

To estimate this, we will use Australia's voting system. Each electorate/county represents 50,000 people and winning majority in an electorate/county awards one point. Since USA's counties have skewed populations, then instead we will consider the entire state's population and award points per 50,000 people. Then the winner will be decided based on majority of points earned.

As a result, Biden wins this scenario around 150 point more than Trump.
